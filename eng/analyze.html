Viewer War

To understand who to sell your cartoon to, you first need to understand who owns the audience. Although Netflix led the revolution, the market is now divided.

Netflix is the volume leader for "Adult Animation". Amazon is a sleeping giant; Disney+ is mostly a "Walled Garden" closed to external IPs (Intellectual Property).

Show Me the Money

1. Cost + Premium (Netflix)

100% of costs + 20% profit. You get money instantly but lose the IP (ownership).

2. License Fee (Amazon/Hulu)

Fixed fee to rent for 1-3 years. IP stays with you but you take the production risk.

3. Revenue Sharing (AVOD)

They pay per view. No upfront money. Good for archive content.

Strategic Recommendation

For a new studio without representation, going directly to Netflix or Disney is almost impossible.

Your Best Move: Prepare a "Promotional Video" (1-2 min) and a strong Presentation File (Pitch Bible). Approach aggregators like FilmHub for Amazon/Tubi access or find a Sales Representative at animation markets (Annecy).

Starting Checklist:

  • Define Target Audience (Kids 6-11 vs Adult)
  • Produce "Proof of Concept"
  • List the Top 10 Sales Representatives in Your Genre
  • Allocate budget for legal advice
